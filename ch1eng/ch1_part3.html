3.1 Measurement of Physical Quantities

🔹 Physical Quantity

Quantities which can be measured or weighed are called physical quantities.

Ex: Time, Work, Mass, Length, Volume, etc.

🔹 Unit

The fixed Standard selected to measure a physical quantity is called its Unit.

📏 Systems of Units:

1. MKS System
  • Length: Meter
  • Mass: Kilogram
  • Time: Second
2. CGS System
  • Length: Centimeter
  • Mass: Gram
  • Time: Second
3. FPS System
  • Length: Foot
  • Mass: Pound
  • Time: Second

🌍 SI System (The International System of Units)

It is the international standard system of units, effectively a modernized version of the MKS System.

2019 UPDATE

🚀 Modern Redefinition of SI Units

Effective May 20, 2019, all 7 base units were redefined mathematically based on exact constant values of 'Natural Constants'.

  • kg (Kilogram) → Based on Planck's Constant (h)
  • A (Ampere) → Based on Elementary Charge (e)
  • K (Kelvin) → Based on Boltzmann Constant (k)
  • mol (Mole) → Based on Avogadro Constant (NA)

3.4 Standard Definitions of Base Units

1. Length (Meter):

The length of the path travelled by light in a vacuum during a time interval of 1/299,792,458 of a second.

2. Mass (Kilogram):

The mass of one liter of pure water at 4°C is considered 1 kg. Now tied to Planck's constant.

3. Time (Second):

The duration of 9,192,631,770 periods of the radiation corresponding to the transition between the two hyperfine levels of the ground state of the Cesium-133 atom.

3.7 Mass and Weight

⚖️ Mass

  • Amount of matter present in a substance is called mass.
  • The mass of a substance is Constant everywhere.
  • SI Unit: Kilogram (kg)
    • 1 kg = 1000 g

🌍 Weight

  • The gravitational force exerted on an object is its weight.
  • Weight may vary from one place to another due to gravity changes.
  • Formula: W = m × g
  • SI Unit: Newton (N)

3.8 Volume & Density

🧊 Volume

The total space occupied by a substance is its volume. SI unit is m³. In chemistry labs, volume is often measured in Liters (L).

1 L = 1000 mL = 1000 cm³ | 1 m³ = 1000 L

💧 Density

Density is defined as the mass per unit volume of a substance. It measures how tightly packed the particles are.

d = m / V
  • It is an intensive property (does not depend on amount).
  • SI Unit: kg/m³
  • Lab Unit: g/cm³
