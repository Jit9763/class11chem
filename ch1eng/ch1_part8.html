Atomic & Molecular Masses

Part 8: Atomic & Molecular Masses

1. Atomic Mass

Atomic mass is the mass of an atom. Since atoms are extremely small, we measure their mass relative to a standard. Currently, **Carbon-12** is used as the standard.

💡 Atomic Mass Unit (amu): Defined as a mass exactly equal to 1/12th of the mass of one Carbon-12 atom.

1 amu = 1.66056 × 10⁻²⁴ g

Nowadays, 'amu' is replaced by 'u' (unified mass) or **Dalton (Da)**.

2. Average Atomic Mass

Many elements exist as mixtures of isotopes. The average atomic mass is calculated by taking into account the natural abundance (%) of each isotope.

Example 1: Isotopes of Carbon

IsotopeAbundance (%)Atomic Mass (u)
¹²C98.89212.000
¹³C1.10813.003
¹⁴C2 × 10⁻¹⁰14.003
Avg Mass = (0.98892 × 12) + (0.01108 × 13.003) + ... ≈ 12.011 u

Example 2: Average Mass of Chlorine

Chlorine exists as Cl-35 and Cl-37 in a 3:1 ratio.

Avg Mass = [(35 × 3) + (37 × 1)] / (3 + 1)
= (105 + 37) / 4 = 142 / 4
Result: 35.5 u

3. Molecular Mass

Molecular mass is the sum of atomic masses of the elements present in a molecule.

Example: Molecular Mass of CO₂
Mass = (Atomic Mass of C) + 2 × (Atomic Mass of O)
= (12.011 u) + 2 × (16.00 u)
= 44.011 u
Example: Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆)
Mass = 6(12.01) + 12(1.008) + 6(16.00)
= 72.06 + 12.09 + 96.00
= 180.15 u
