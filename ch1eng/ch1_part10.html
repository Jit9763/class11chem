Part 10: Percentage Composition & Formulas

10.1 Percentage Composition

Percentage composition is the percentage by mass of each element in a compound. It is vital for identifying unknown substances.

Mass % of an Element =
(Mass of that element in compound) / (Molar mass of compound) × 100

Example: % of Na and Cl in NaCl

Molar mass of NaCl = 23 (Na) + 35.5 (Cl) = 58.5 g/mol

  • 🔹 **Mass % of Na** = (23 / 58.5) × 100 = **39.31 %**
  • 🔹 **Mass % of Cl** = (35.5 / 58.5) × 100 = **60.69 %**

10.2 Empirical & Molecular Formulas

📌 Empirical Formula

The simplest whole-number ratio of various atoms present in a compound.

📌 Molecular Formula

The actual number of atoms of various elements present in a molecule of a compound.

CompoundMolecular FormulaEmpirical Formula
GlucoseC₆H₁₂O₆CH₂O
BenzeneC₆H₆CH
EthyneC₂H₂CH

Relationship:

Molecular Formula = n × Empirical Formula

Where n = (Molecular Mass) / (Empirical Formula Mass)

Solved Problem: Determination of Formula

Q: A compound contains 4.07% H, 24.27% C and 71.65% Cl. Its molar mass is 98.96 g. Find its formula.

Step 1: In 100g, we have 4.07g H, 24.27g C, 71.65g Cl.
Step 2: Moles: H = 4.04, C = 2.02, Cl = 2.02.
Step 3: Divide by smallest value (2.02): H=2, C=1, Cl=1.
Step 4: **Empirical Formula = CH₂Cl**
Step 5: Empirical Mass = 49.48 g.
Step 6: n = 98.96 / 49.48 = **2**.
Final: Molecular Formula = (CH₂Cl) × 2 = C₂H₄Cl₂
