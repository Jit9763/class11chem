10.1 Stoichiometry (रससमीकरणमिति)

Stoichiometry deals with the calculation of masses (and sometimes volumes) of the reactants and the products involved in a chemical reaction.

रासायनिक अभिक्रियाओं में क्रियाकारकों एवं उत्पादों के मध्य मात्रात्मक सम्बन्धों का ज्ञान 'रससमीकरणमिति' कहलाता है।
🇬🇷 Greek Origin: Stoicheion (Element) + Metron (Measure)

Key Principles of Stoichiometry

  • Always start with a Balanced Chemical Equation.
  • The coefficients in a balanced equation are called Stoichiometric Coefficients.
  • These coefficients represent the number of moles of each substance.

💡 The 4 Golden Rules of Stoichiometry

1 Write the Balanced Chemical Equation.
2 Convert given mass or volume into Moles.
3 Use the Molar Ratio from the equation to find unknown moles.
4 Convert the result back to Mass or Volume as required.

🔥 Example: Combustion of Methane

1 CH₄(g) + 2 O₂(g) ⟶ 1 CO₂(g) + 2 H₂O(g)

According to the coefficients:
1 mole of CH₄ reacts with 2 moles of O₂ to produce 1 mole of CO₂ and 2 moles of H₂O.

📝 Problem: Calculate the mass of water (H₂O) produced by the combustion of 16g of methane (CH₄).

1. Moles of CH₄: 16g / 16 g/mol = 1 Mole

2. Equation: 1 mole CH₄ ⟶ 2 moles H₂O

3. Mass of H₂O: 2 moles × 18 g/mol

Final Answer = 36 grams of H₂O

10.2 Limiting Reagent (सीमान्त अभिकर्मक)

🥪 The Sandwich Analogy

To make 1 sandwich, you need 2 bread slices + 1 cheese slice.

If you have 10 bread slices but only 2 cheese slices, you can ONLY make 2 sandwiches.

Here, Cheese is the Limiting Reagent because it ran out first and limited the production. Bread is the Excess Reagent.

The reactant which is completely consumed first in a reaction is called the Limiting Reagent. It determines the maximum amount of product that can be formed.

वह अभिकारक जो पहले समाप्त हो जाता है, वह उत्पाद की मात्रा को भी सीमित कर देता है इसलिए इसे सीमान्त अभिकर्मक कहते हैं।

💡 Example: Formation of Water from H₂ and O₂

2 H₂ (g) + O₂ (g) ⟶ 2 H₂O (l)

If we have 2 moles of H₂ and 2 moles of O₂:
- The equation says 2 moles of H₂ need only 1 mole of O₂.
- So, 1 mole of O₂ will be left over (Excess).
- H₂ will be completely consumed first.

Therefore, Hydrogen (H₂) is the Limiting Reagent.
