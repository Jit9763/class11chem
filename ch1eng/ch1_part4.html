4.1 Scientific Notation

In chemistry, we deal with extremely large or tiny numbers. To make them readable, we use **Scientific Notation**, expressed as **N × 10ⁿ**.

Example: Avogadro No.

6.022 × 10²³

Example: Mass of H-atom

1.66 × 10⁻²⁴ g

4.2 Significant Figures

"Significant figures are meaningful digits which are known with certainty plus one which is estimated or uncertain."

📏 Rules for Determining Significant Figures:

Rule 1

All non-zero digits are significant.

2478 → 4
34125 → 5
Rule 2

Zeros between two non-zero digits are significant.

2024 → 4
90205 → 5
Rule 3

Zeros to the left of the first non-zero digit are NOT significant.

0.00486 → 3
0.01 → 1
Rule 4

In numbers without a decimal, trailing zeros are usually NOT significant.

4500 → 2
2050 → 3
Rule 5

Trailing zeros in a number with a decimal are significant.

2.00 → 3
1230.0 → 5
Rule 6

Counting numbers have infinite (∞) significant figures.

2 balls → ∞
50 eggs → ∞

➡️ Rounding Off

1. If the last digit < 5: Preceding digit remains unchanged. (Ex: 12.862 → 12.86)

2. If the last digit > 5: Preceding digit increases by 1. (Ex: 12.868 → 12.87)

3. If the last digit is exactly 5: If preceding digit is Even, keep it same. If Odd, add 1.
(Ex: 4.25 → 4.2 | 27.75 → 27.8)
