Part 12: Final Review & Chapter Summary

Summary of Key Concepts

We have covered everything from the basic nature of matter to complex calculations of molarity and stoichiometry. Here is a quick wrap-up:

  • ✅ Law of Conservation of Mass: Mass remains constant in a reaction.
  • ✅ Mole Concept: 1 mole = 6.022 × 10²³ particles = Molar Mass.
  • ✅ Limiting Reagent: The reactant that runs out first.
  • ✅ Molarity (M): Moles of solute per litre of solution.
  • ✅ Molality (m): Moles of solute per kg of solvent.

Practice Questions for Revision

Q1: Why is molality preferred over molarity for high-precision temperature experiments?

Ans: Molarity depends on volume, which changes with temperature. Molality depends on mass, which remains constant regardless of temperature.

Q2: Calculate the number of atoms in 52 moles of Argon.

Ans: 52 × 6.022 × 10²³ = 3.131 × 10²⁵ atoms.

Q3: What is the unit of Molarity?

Ans: mol L⁻¹ (or M).
