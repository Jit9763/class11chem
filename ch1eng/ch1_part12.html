12.1 Laws of Chemical Combination

1. Law of Conservation of Mass

Matter can neither be created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction. (Lavoisier)

द्रव्यमान संरक्षण का नियम: द्रव्य को न तो बनाया जा सकता है और न ही नष्ट किया जा सकता है।

2. Law of Definite Proportions

A pure compound always contains the same elements combined in the same fixed ratio by mass. (Proust)

स्थिर अनुपात का नियम: किसी शुद्ध यौगिक में तत्वों के द्रव्यमानों का अनुपात सदैव स्थिर रहता है।

3. Law of Multiple Proportions

When two elements form more than one compound, the masses of one element that combine with a fixed mass of the other are in a simple whole-number ratio. (Dalton)

गुणित अनुपात का नियम: एक तत्व का भिन्न द्रव्यमान जो दूसरे के निश्चित द्रव्यमान से सहयोग करता है, सरल अनुपात में होता है।

4. Gay Lussac's Law of Gaseous Volumes

When gases react, they do so in volumes which bear a simple ratio to one another (at constant T and P).

गे-लुसैक का नियम: गैसों के आयतनों में एक सरल अनुपात होता है।

5. Avogadro's Law

Equal volumes of all gases at the same temperature and pressure contain the same number of molecules.

आवोगाद्रो का नियम: गैसों के समान आयतनों में अणुओं की संख्या समान होती है।

12.2 Master Solved Numericals

📝 Question 1: Calculate the molarity of NaOH in the solution prepared by dissolving its 4g in enough water to form 250 mL of the solution.

Solution:

1. Moles of NaOH =
Given Mass (4g) Molar Mass (40g/mol)
= 0.1 mol
2. Volume of solution = 250 mL = 0.250 Liters
3. Molarity (M) =
Moles of Solute Volume of solution (L)
=
0.1 mol 0.250 L
Final Answer = 0.4 mol/L (0.4 M)
📝 Question 2: The density of 3 M solution of NaCl is 1.25 g/mL. Calculate the molality of the solution.

Solution:

1. Molarity = 3 mol/L. This means 3 moles of NaCl in 1L of solution.
2. Mass of NaCl = 3 mol × 58.5 g/mol = 175.5 g
3. Mass of Solution = Vol × Density = 1000 mL × 1.25 g/mL = 1250 g
4. Mass of Solvent (Water) = 1250g - 175.5g = 1074.5 g = 1.0745 kg
5. Molality (m) =
Moles of Solute Mass of Solvent (kg)
=
3 mol 1.0745 kg
Final Answer = 2.79 m
