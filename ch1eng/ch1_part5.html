Dimensional Analysis

Part 5: Dimensional Analysis

Definition & Principle

When calculating, often units must be converted from one system to another. The method used to accomplish this is called **Factor Label Method** or **Unit Factor Method** or **Dimensional Analysis**.

💡 Note: This method relies on multipying the given value by a 'Unit Factor' (a ratio that equals 1) so that the unwanted units cancel out.

Important Conversion Factors

1 Calorie (Cal)= 4.186 J
1 Angstrom (Å)= 10⁻¹⁰ m
1 Dyne= 10⁻⁵ N
1 Micron (μm)= 10⁻⁶ m
1 Erg= 10⁻⁷ J
1 Nanometer (nm)= 10⁻⁹ m

Solved Examples (Numerical)

Example 1: Å to pm conversion

Q: Convert 0.50 Å into picometers (pm).

1 Å = 10⁻¹⁰ m
1 pm = 10⁻¹² m
0.50 Å = (0.50 × 10⁻¹⁰ m) × (1 pm / 10⁻¹² m)
= 0.50 × 10² pm
Ans: 50 pm

Example 2: Inch to cm conversion

Q: A piece of metal is 3 inches long. What is its length in cm?

1 inch = 2.54 cm
Unit Factor = (2.54 cm / 1 inch)
3 inches = 3 × 2.54 cm
Ans: 7.62 cm

Example 3: Litre to m³ conversion

Q: A jug contains 2L of milk. Calculate its volume in m³.

1 L = 1000 cm³
1 m³ = 10⁶ cm³
Unit Factor = (1 m³ / 10⁶ cm³)
2L = 2000 cm³ × (1 m³ / 10⁶ cm³)
= 2 × 10⁻³ m³
Ans: 2 × 10⁻³ m³

Example 4: Time conversion

Q: How many seconds are there in 2 days?

2 days × (24 h / 1 day) × (60 min / 1 h) × (60 s / 1 min)
= 2 × 24 × 60 × 60 seconds
Ans: 1,72,800 s
