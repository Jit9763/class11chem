Dalton's Atomic Theory

Part 7: Dalton's Atomic Theory

In 1808, John Dalton published "A New System of Chemical Philosophy" in which he proposed the atomic theory. This theory was based on the laws of chemical combination.

Main Points of Dalton’s Theory:

❶

Matter consists of indivisible particles called Atoms.

❷

All atoms of a given element have **identical** properties, including identical mass.

❸

Atoms of different elements differ in mass.

❹

Compounds are formed when atoms of different elements combine in a **fixed ratio**.

❺

Chemical reactions involve reorganization of atoms. These are neither created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction.

Relating Vapor Density & Molecular Weight

Vapor Density: The ratio of the weight of a certain volume of a gas to the weight of an equal volume of hydrogen under the same conditions.

Molecular Weight = 2 × Vapor Density

Note: This is derived using Avogadro’s Hypothesis and the fact that Hydrogen is diatomic (H₂).

Limitations of Dalton’s Theory:

  • ❌ It could not explain Gay Lussac’s Law of gaseous volumes.
  • ❌ It failed to explain why atoms of different elements differ in mass and valency.
  • ❌ Isotopes & Isobars: Dalton stated all atoms of an element are same, but later discovery of **isotopes** (atoms of same element with different mass) proved this wrong.
