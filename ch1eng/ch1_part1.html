Part 1: Detailed Introduction and Applications of Chemistry

1.1 Science

The systematic and organized knowledge of nature is called Science (विज्ञान). Science has various branches such as Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Geology, etc.

1.2 Chemistry

That branch of science in which the structure, properties, and interactions (अन्योन्य क्रियाओं) of substances are studied is called Chemistry. Chemistry is also called "Central Science" because it connects Physics, Biology, and Geology.

📜 Origin of the Word & Extensive History:

The word 'Chemistry' evolved from the ancient Egyptian word 'Chemia', which means 'Black Color' (काला रंग). Because of this black color of Egypt's fertile soil, the ancient art was called 'Chemia'.

The Concept of 'Chemitaking': In ancient times, the art of making Alloys (मिश्रित धातुओं) by mixing copper, gold, and silver with other substances was called Chemitaking. It was this art that later developed into modern Chemistry.

(Note: Robert Boyle is considered one of the founders of modern chemistry, while Antoine Lavoisier is called the 'Father of Chemistry'.)

1.3 Major Branches of Chemistry:

1. Inorganic Chemistry (अकार्बनिक रसायन)
2. Organic Chemistry (कार्बनिक रसायन)
3. Physical Chemistry (भौतिक रसायन)
4. Analytical Chemistry (विश्लेषणात्मक रसायन)
5. Biochemistry (जैव रसायन): Study of processes like Photosynthesis and Respiration occurring in plants and animals.
6. Nuclear Chemistry (नाभिकीय रसायन): Study of Atomic Bombs and Radioactive Isotopes.
7. Medicinal Chemistry (चिकित्सा रसायन): The science of synthesizing new drugs for the treatment of severe diseases.
8. Environmental Chemistry (पर्यावरण रसायन): Study of control of air, water, and soil pollution and environmental conservation.
9. Industrial Chemistry (औद्योगिक रसायन): Techniques for large-scale production of chemicals.

1.4 Use of Chemistry in Various Fields:

1. In the Field of Agriculture:

  • In the production of chemical fertilizers.
  • In the manufacturing of various insecticides, fungicides, and Pesticides (पीड़कनाशकों).

2. In Industries (Industrial Impact):

The development of industries like cement, paper, leather, dye, glass, paint, soap, detergents, synthetic fibers, metal extraction, photography, and petroleum refining depends on chemistry. It is the backbone of the modern economy.

3. Health and Medicine (Medicinal Revolution):

  • Cancer: Life-saving drugs used for its treatment - Cisplatin and Taxol.
  • AIDS: Successful creation of the drug Azidothymidine (AZT) to provide relief to AIDS patients.
  • Thyroid: Use of radioactive isotopes like Iodine-131 (I-131) in the testing and treatment of the thyroid gland.
  • Contribution of Ether and Chloroform used for Anaesthesia (संज्ञाहरण) in medical science.

4. In Defense and Warfare (National Defense):

Chemistry has played a crucial role in national security. This includes the manufacturing of powerful explosives:

  • High-capacity explosives like TNT (Trinitrotoluene) and Dynamite.
  • Complex structures of the Atomic Bomb and Hydrogen Bomb.
  • Poisonous Gases: Mustard Gas used during World War I and Chloropicrin used as Tear Gas (अक्षि-विष).

🚀 NEET/JEE Added Notes: Part 1

  • Cisplatin: [Pt(NH₃)₂Cl₂] - Mainly used for testicular and ovarian cancer treatment.
  • AZT (Azidothymidine): Also called 'Zidovudine'. It stops the replication of the HIV/AIDS virus.
  • Iodine-131: This isotope is used for thyroid cancer treatment and for diagnosing Goitre (घेँघा) disease.
  • Mnemonics: Remember 'C-T-M' (Chemistry, Technology, Medicine) - Modern healthcare is impossible without chemistry.
