Part 9: Mole Concept & Molar Mass

9.1 The Mole - A Basic Unit

Amount of substance is a base physical quantity in the S.I. system, and its unit is the **Mole (mol)**.

Definitions of 1 Mole:

🔹 Based on Carbon-12:

"The amount of a substance that contains as many particles (atoms, molecules, or ions) as there are atoms in exactly 12 g of the Carbon-12 isotope."

Avogadro's Number (Nₐ) = 6.022 × 10²³

🔹 Based on Mass:

The amount of a substance whose mass in grams is equal to its atomic or molecular mass.

🔹 Molar Volume (Gases):

At STP (Standard Temperature and Pressure), 1 mole of any gas occupies a volume of **22.4 L**.

📌 Key Formulas

  • ✅ 1 Mole = Gram Atomic/Molecular Mass = 6.022 × 10²³ particles
  • ✅ Number of Moles (n) = (Given Mass in grams) / (Molar Mass)
  • ✅ Number of Particles = Moles (n) × Avogadro's Number (Nₐ)
  • ✅ Number of Moles (for Gas) = (Volume at STP in Litres) / 22.4 L

Solved Examples

1 Mass of 1 Molecule of CO₂

Molar Mass of CO₂ = 44 g/mol
Mass of 6.022 × 10²³ molecules = 44 g
Mass of 1 molecule = 44 / (6.022 × 10²³)
= 7.306 × 10⁻²³ g

2 Moles in 36g of Water (H₂O)

Molar Mass of H₂O = 18 g/mol
Moles (n) = Given Mass / Molar Mass
Moles = 36 / 18
= 2 mol

3 Molecules in 11.2 L of CO₂ at STP

Moles (n) = 11.2 / 22.4 = 0.5 mol
Molecules = 0.5 × 6.022 × 10²³
= 3.011 × 10²³ molecules
