Part 6: Laws of Chemical Combination (रासायनिक संयोजन के नियम)

Various elements combine to form compounds. There are five fundamental laws that govern these combinations:

1. Law of Conservation of Mass
(द्रव्यमान संरक्षण का नियम)

🔹 Proposed by Antoine Lavoisier (लेवोसिए).

"Matter can neither be created nor destroyed. The total mass of reactants taking part in a chemical reaction is equal to the total mass of the products formed."
Example: Combustion of Carbon (कार्बन का दहन) C + O₂ → CO₂
12g + 32g → 44g
(Total Reactants = 44g | Total Products = 44g)

2. Law of Definite Proportions
(स्थिर अनुपात का नियम)

🔹 Proposed by French chemist Joseph Proust (प्राऊस्ट).

"In a chemical compound, the elements are always present in a definite proportion by mass, regardless of the source from which it is obtained."
Example: Water (H₂O)
In water, the ratio of the mass of hydrogen to the mass of oxygen is always 2:16 or 1:8.

3. Law of Multiple Proportions
(गुणित अनुपात का नियम)

🔹 Proposed by John Dalton (डॉल्टन).

"When two elements combine to form more than one compound, the masses of one element that combine with a fixed mass of the other element are in a simple whole-number ratio."
Example: Carbon & Oxygen (CO and CO₂)
• Ratio in CO: 12g C combines with 16g O
• Ratio in CO₂: 12g C combines with 32g O
Ratio of Oxygen combined with fixed Carbon: 16:32 = 1 : 2

4. Gay-Lussac’s Law of Gaseous Volumes
(गे-लूसैक का गैसीय आयतनों का नियम)

"When gases react together or are produced in a chemical reaction at constant temperature and pressure, they do so in a simple whole-number ratio by volume."
Example: Formation of Water Vapor
2H₂ (g) + O₂ (g) → 2H₂O (g)
(2 vol.) + (1 vol.) → (2 vol.)
200ml : 100ml : 200ml
RATIO = 2 : 1 : 2

5. Avogadro’s Law
(आवोगाद्रो का नियम)

"Equal volumes of all gases at the same temperature and pressure should contain equal number of molecules."
