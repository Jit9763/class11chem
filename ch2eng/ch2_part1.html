2.1 Introduction to Atomic Structure

Early History of Atom

• Maharishi Kanad & Democritus (400 BC): They first proposed that matter is made up of extremely small particles. The word "Atom" comes from the Greek word 'a-tomio', which means 'Uncuttable' or 'Indivisible'.

• Dalton's Atomic Theory (1808): John Dalton also considered the atom as the ultimate indivisible particle of matter, which can neither be created nor destroyed.

कणाद एवं डेमोक्रिटस के अनुसार परमाणु अविभाज्य (Uncuttable) है। डाल्टन ने भी इसे अविभाज्य माना।

2.2 Discovery of Electron: Cathode Rays

In 1897, J.J. Thomson and William Crookes performed the Cathode Ray Discharge Tube experiment which ultimately led to the discovery of the electron.

थॉमसन और क्रूक्स ने कैथोड किरण विसर्जन नलिका (Discharge Tube) प्रयोग किया।

Experimental Conditions:

  • High Voltage: Approximately 10,000 V
  • Very Low Pressure: Approximately 110000 atm (maintained via vacuum pump)

Under these specific conditions, invisible rays began to travel from the Cathode (Negative) to the Anode (Positive). When the glass wall behind the anode was coated with a fluorescent material like Zinc Sulphide (ZnS), a bright glow was observed. These rays were named Cathode Rays.

इन शर्तों (उच्च वोल्टेज और निम्न दाब) पर कैथोड से ऐनोड की ओर किरणें निकलती हैं जो ZnS पर्दे पर चमक उत्पन्न करती हैं।
Cathode Rays = Negatively Charged Particles = Electrons

Key Properties of Cathode Rays

  • They travel in straight lines.
  • They consist of material particles (capable of rotating a light paddle wheel placed in their path).
  • In the presence of an Electric or Magnetic field, they deflect towards the positive plate, which proves that they are negatively charged.
  • The nature of cathode rays is independent of the material of the electrodes or the nature of the gas present in the tube.
मुख्य गुण: 1. सीधी रेखा में चलती हैं। 2. छोटे कणों से बनी हैं। 3. ऋणावेशित (Negative) होती हैं। 4. धातु या गैस पर निर्भर नहीं करतीं।
