2.7 Atomic Models: A New Mystery (परमाणु मॉडल)

Once it was established that both electrons (-) and protons (+) exist inside an atom, a massive question arose before scientists: "How are these particles exactly arranged (व्यवस्थित) inside the atom?"

Since minus and plus charges attract each other, do they stick together? Or do they stay apart? To solve this mystery, several scientists proposed their own atomic models (परमाणु मॉडल).

जब यह साबित हो गया कि परमाणु के अंदर इलेक्ट्रॉन (-) और प्रोटॉन (+) दोनों मौजूद हैं, तो वैज्ञानिकों के सामने यह सवाल खड़ा हो गया कि वे आपस में किस तरह व्यवस्थित हैं?

2.8 Thomson's Model of Atom (थॉमसन का परमाणु मॉडल)

In 1898, J.J. Thomson, the discoverer of electrons, proposed the very first model of an atom. He beautifully explained it using an analogy known as the Plum Pudding Model or the Watermelon Model (तरबूज मॉडल).

The Watermelon Analogy (तरबूज का उदाहरण):

  • The atom is a positively charged sphere. Thomson compared this to the red edible part (लाल भाग) of the watermelon.
  • The electrons are embedded inside this positive sphere, just like the black seeds (काले बीज) are distributed in a watermelon!
  • This model successfully explained that the total positive charge equals the total negative charge, making the entire atom electrically neutral (उदासीन).

Limitation of Thomson's Model (विफलता):

Although this model perfectly explained electrical neutrality, it completely failed to explain the results of future experiments (especially Rutherford's gold foil experiment). Consequently, this model was rejected!

2.9 Rutherford's Scattering Experiment (रदरफोर्ड का प्रयोग)

The credit for revealing the true structure of an atom goes to Ernest Rutherford. In 1911, he performed a highly famous experiment that proved to be a milestone in the history of science!

Experimental Setup (प्रयोग की व्यवस्था):

  • Rutherford took an exceptionally thin Gold Foil (सोने की पन्नी), which was only 100 nanometers (nm) thick.
  • This foil was heavily bombarded with heavy and fast-moving Alpha (α) particles from a radioactive source. Alpha particles carry a positive (+) charge.
  • A circular fluorescent screen made of Zinc Sulfide (ZnS) was placed around the foil. Whenever an alpha particle struck this screen, a tiny flash (चमक) of light was produced.
रदरफोर्ड ने सोने की अत्यंत पतली पन्नी पर पॉजिटिव अल्फा (α) कणों की बमबारी की और पता लगाने के लिए चारों ओर ज़िंक सल्फाइड की स्क्रीन लगा दी।

Astounding Observations (तथ्य / परिणाम):

According to Thomson's model, the heavy alpha particles should have easily passed straight through the soft positive sphere. But what actually happened shocked everyone!

99%
Passed Straight (सीधे निकल गए):
The vast majority of the alpha (α) particles passed straight through the gold foil without any deflection or disturbance.
~ 1%
Small Deflections (मुड़ गए):
A small fraction of alpha particles got deflected (bent) by small angles while passing through the foil.
1 in 20,000
Bounced Back! (वापस लौट आए):
Out of 20,000 particles, almost 1 particle suffered a 180° deflection, meaning it bounced straight back! It was as shocking as an artillery shell bouncing off tissue paper.

Rutherford's Conclusions (निष्कर्ष):

  • (i) Empty Space (खाली स्थान): Since 99% of particles passed straight through, Rutherford concluded that most of the space inside an atom is entirely empty!
  • (ii) Discovery of Nucleus (नाभिक की खोज): The deflected and rebounded particles proved that all the positive (+) charge and almost all the mass (द्रव्यमान) of the atom are heavily concentrated in a tiny dense region exactly in the center. Rutherford named this dense center the Nucleus (नाभिक)!
