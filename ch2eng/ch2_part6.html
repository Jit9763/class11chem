2.12 Atomic Number and Mass Number (परमाणु संख्या और द्रव्यमान संख्या)

After Rutherford's experiment, it became clear that the entire positive charge and almost all the mass (द्रव्यमान) of an atom are locked inside the nucleus. True identity of any element depends on the number of protons and neutrons located deep inside its nucleus.

1. Atomic Number (परमाणु संख्या, Z)

  • The total number of protons (p) present in the nucleus is known as the atomic number (Z).
  • In a neutral atom (उदासीन परमाणु), the number of protons is exactly equal to the number of electrons.
    • Atomic Number (Z) = No. of Protons = No. of Electrons

2. Mass Number (द्रव्यमान संख्या, A)

  • The sum total of the number of protons (p) and neutrons (n) present in the nucleus is called the mass number (A).
  • Protons and neutrons are collectively known as Nucleons (न्यूक्लियॉन)!
    • Mass Number (A) = Protons (p) + Neutrons (n)

Representation of an Atom (परमाणु को दर्शाने का तरीका):

A Z
X

Where X = Symbol of element, Z = Atomic Number (subscript), A = Mass Number (superscript).

किसी भी तत्व को दर्शाने के लिए उसकी परमाणु संख्या (Z) नीचे की तरफ और द्रव्यमान संख्या (A) ऊपर की तरफ लिखी जाती है।

2.13 Isotopes and Isobars (समस्थानिक व समभारिक)

1. Isotopes (समस्थानिक)

Atoms of the same element that have the same Atomic Number (Z) but different Mass Numbers (A) are called isotopes.

(Since Z is same, their chemical properties are absolutely identical!)

3 Isotopes of Hydrogen (हाइड्रोजन के समस्थानिक):
11H
Protium
21H
Deuterium
31H
Tritium

2. Isobars (समभारिक)

Atoms of different elements that have the same Mass Number (A) but different Atomic Numbers (Z) are called isobars.

(Since their Z is different, their chemical properties are completely different!)

Example: Argon and Calcium
4018Ar
and
4020Ca

Both have a massive weight of 40! (सम-भार)
