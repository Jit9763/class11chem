2.3 Charge to Mass Ratio (आवेश-द्रव्यमान अनुपात) of Electron

Since electrons are so incredibly small that their mass (द्रव्यमान) cannot be measured directly on a scale, scientists used a clever trick! In 1897, J.J. Thomson decided to measure the ratio of their electrical charge (विद्युत आवेश, e) to their mass (द्रव्यमान, me) using a modified Cathode Ray Tube (कैथोड किरण नलिका).

इलेक्ट्रॉन के द्रव्यमान को सीधे तोलना संभव नहीं था, इसलिए थॉमसन ने आवेश और द्रव्यमान का अनुपात (Ratio) निकाला।

How did Thomson do it? (The Experiment):

  • He applied both a Magnetic Field (चुंबकीय क्षेत्र) and an Electric Field (विद्युत क्षेत्र) perpendicular (लंबवत) to the path of the cathode rays (electrons).
  • The magnetic field deflected (मोड़ दिया) them in one direction, while the electric field deflected them in the opposite direction. Notice that lighter electrons deflect more, and heavier ones deflect less.
  • Thomson perfectly 'balanced' (संतुलित) both forces so that the electrons traveled in a straight line again!

By solving the mathematics of this balancing act, Thomson obtained a fixed value for the e/m ratio.

e me
= 1.758820 × 1011 C/kg

2.4 Charge on Electron (इलेक्ट्रॉन पर आवेश)

Thomson provided the ratio, but what is the exact charge of a single electron? The precise answer was found by R.A. Millikan in 1909 through his brilliant Oil Drop Experiment (तैल बूँद प्रयोग).

What did Millikan do?

  • He sprayed a very fine mist of oil drops (तेल की बूँदें) into a closed chamber using an atomizer (फुहार यंत्र).
  • He bombarded the air inside with X-rays (एक्स-रे), tearing out electrons from the gas. The falling oil droplets swallowed (captured) these electrons and became negatively charged (ऋणावेशित).
  • As these oil drops fell due to gravity (गुरुत्वाकर्षण), Millikan turned on a powerful positive plate (Electric field) at the top, which began pulling the negative droplets upwards.
  • By carefully adjusting the voltage, he suspended (हवा में रोक दिया) the drops in mid-air! At this point, the downward gravitational force perfectly matched the upward electric force.
मिलिकन ने तेल की कुछ बूँदों (Oil Drops) को एक्स-रे की मदद से आवेशित (Negative) किया, और फिर इलेक्ट्रिक फील्ड लगाकर उन्हें हवा में टांग दिया।

The Magic Conclusion:

After measuring thousands of suspended drops, Millikan found that the charge on every single drop was always an integral multiple of a specific 'fixed number'. This fundamental fixed number was the charge of a single electron!

e = 1.6022 × 10-19 C

प्रत्येक बूँद पर आवेश हमेशा एक 'निश्चित मान' का ही गुणज (Multiple) था, जो कि 1.6022 × 10-19 C था।

Mass of Electron (इलेक्ट्रॉन का द्रव्यमान) - The Final Calculation

Now we had both pieces of the puzzle! Thomson had found the e/m ratio, and Millikan had found the absolute charge e. By applying a simple mathematical trick of dividing (भाग) these values, scientists were able to weigh the electron without ever putting it on a scale!

Step-by-Step Maths:

Step 1: Setting up the Formula

me = Millikan's Charge (e) Thomson's Ratio (e / me)

Step 2: Substituting Values

= 1.6022 × 10-19 C 1.758820 × 1011 C/kg

Step 3: Solving Exponents

When we move the 1011 from the denominator to the numerator, it becomes 10-11. We then add the powers: (-19) + (-11) = -30

= 1.6022 1.758820 × 10-30 kg

Step 4: Final Division

Dividing 1.6022 by 1.758820 gives approximately 0.91094.

= 0.91094 × 10-30 kg

Following the rules of Scientific Notation, we shift the decimal point one place to the right, which subtracts exactly 1 from the exponent (-30 becomes -31):

me = 9.1094 × 10-31 kg
वैज्ञानिक संकेतन (Scientific Notation) के नियमों को लागू करते हुए इलेक्ट्रॉन का द्रव्यमान (Mass) 9.1094 × 10-31 kg प्राप्त होता है।
