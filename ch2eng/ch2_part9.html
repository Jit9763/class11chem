2.17 Particle Nature of Radiation (विकिरण की कणीय प्रकृति)

Maxwell's Wave Theory was excellent, but it completely failed to explain certain physical phenomena such as Black Body Radiation and the Photoelectric Effect.

Planck's Quantum Theory - 1900 (प्लांक का क्वांटम सिद्धांत):

To overcome these shortcomings, the great physicist Max Planck introduced a revolutionary new theory in 1900. According to his theory:

"Radiant energy is emitted or absorbed not continuously, but discontinuously in the form of small discrete packets or bundles of energy."

🌟 The smallest quantity of this energy packet is called a "Quantum (क्वांटम)". (In the case of light, it is called a "Photon (फोटोन)"!)

2.18 Energy of a Quantum (क्वांटम की ऊर्जा)

Planck stated that the Energy (E) of a quantum of radiation is directly proportional to its Frequency (ν).

This means: the higher the Frequency, the more Energy the packet contains!

E ∝ ν

By removing the proportionality sign, a constant 'h' is introduced:

E = hν
  • E = Energy of a Quantum/Photon
  • ν = Frequency of the radiation
  • h = Planck's Constant
Value of 'h': 6.626 × 10-34 J s

Since we learned earlier that (ν = c / λ), we can substitute this into the equation:

E = hc λ
