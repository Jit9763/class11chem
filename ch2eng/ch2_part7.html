2.14 Nature of Light (प्रकाश की प्रकृति)

Before we can overcome the drawbacks of Rutherford's model and move on to Bohr's revolutionary model, we must first understand the true nature of light (प्रकाश). Historically, Newton believed light consisted of tiny particles.

However, in 1870, the brilliant physicist James Clerk Maxwell proposed a completely new and shocking theory to the world—known as the Electromagnetic Wave Theory (विद्युत चुम्बकीय तरंग सिद्धांत).

Maxwell's Theory (मैक्सवेल का सिद्धांत):

  • Whenever an electrically charged particle (like an electron) moves under acceleration (त्वरण), alternating electrical and magnetic fields are produced.
  • These fields are transmitted outwards in the forms of Waves (तरंगें).
  • Since they contain both Electric and Magnetic fields, they are called Electromagnetic Radiations (EMR). Light itself is an EMR!

2.15 Characteristics of EM Waves (विद्युत चुम्बकीय तरंगों की विशेषताएँ)

1. Perpendicular Fields (लंबवत क्षेत्र)

The oscillating Electric field and Magnetic field are mutually perpendicular ($90^{\circ}$) to each other. Furthermore, both fields are perpendicular to the direction of propagation (आगे बढ़ने की दिशा) of the wave.

2. No Medium Req. (बिना माध्यम यात्रा)

Unlike sound waves or water waves, EM waves do not require any medium to travel. They can easily travel through empty space or Vacuum (निर्वात). (This is how sunlight reaches Earth!)

3. Speed of Light (प्रकाश की गति)

In a vacuum, all types of electromagnetic waves travel at the exact same constant speed. This speed is 3 × 108 m/s (or 300,000 km per second), which is denoted by the letter 'c'.
