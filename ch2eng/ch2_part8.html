2.16 Properties of Waves (तरंगों के गुण)

Since light and other Electromagnetic Radiations travel as waves (तरंगें), we must familiarize ourselves with some fundamental characteristics used to describe them:

1. Wavelength (तरंगदैर्ध्य) λ

It is defined as the distance between any two consecutive Crests (शिखर) or any two consecutive Troughs (गर्त) of a wave.

Symbol: Lambda (λ)
Unit: Metre (m) or Angstrom (Å)
(1 Å = 10-10 m)

2. Frequency (आवृत्ति) ν

It is defined as the total number of waves that pass through a given point in one second.

Symbol: Nu (ν)
Unit: Hertz (Hz) or s-1

3. Velocity (वेग) c

It is defined as the distance travelled by a wave in one second. All electromagnetic waves travel at the speed of light.

Symbol: c
Value: 3 × 108 m s-1

4. Wavenumber (तरंग संख्या) ν

It is defined as the number of wavelengths per unit length. Mathematically, it is the exact reciprocal (उल्टा) of wavelength.

Formula: ν = 1 λ
Unit: m-1 or cm-1

The Grand Relation (इनके बीच का महा-संबंध)

Since the velocity ($c$) of all EM waves is fixed, as the Frequency ($\nu$) increases, the Wavelength ($\lambda$) has to decrease. They are completely inversely proportional to each other!

c = ν × λ
